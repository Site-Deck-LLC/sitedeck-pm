# Product

## Register

product

## Users

EPC contractors managing utility-scale construction projects (BESS, substation, transmission, solar) in the $1M–$50M range with teams of 5–100 people.

Primary users:
- **Project Managers** — own the schedule, cost baseline, and change control; live in the morning dashboard and Gantt views.
- **Superintendents** — consume schedule status and material alerts via SiteDeck Pro; do not log into PM directly.
- **Owner's Reps** — read-only access to dashboard tiles (no cost), issue tracker, and RFI status.
- **Subcontractor PMs** — scoped to their own subcontract, schedule window, and submittals only.

Users are often on job-site tablets or laptops in bright, noisy, time-constrained conditions. Interfaces must read at a glance and require zero training.

## Product Purpose

SiteDeck PM is the command layer of the SiteDeck portfolio. It owns the project registry, schedule, cost, procurement, scope, communications, and risk for EPC contractors. Data flows down to SiteDeck Pro (field operations) via webhooks; field activity flows back up to update earned value, delivery status, and cost incurred.

Success looks like: a PM opens the app at 6:30 AM, scans six dashboard tiles, knows exactly what needs attention today, and drills into the problem in under 30 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Intuitive. Field-ready. Decisive.

- **Intuitive:** No manuals. Every screen answers "what do I do now?" on first glance.
- **Field-ready:** Information density tuned for outdoor, high-glare, gloved-hand, interrupted contexts. Touch targets large. Status color-coding unambiguous.
- **Decisive:** The interface surfaces decisions and their owners, not just data. Red means "someone needs to act now." Green means "move on."

## Anti-references

- **Not construction clip art.** No hard hats, cranes, or blueprint textures as decoration. The product is a tool, not a brochure.
- **Not startup SaaS.** No hero metrics, gradient text, "supercharge your workflow" copy, or generic SaaS-cream minimalism. Density and clarity matter more than whitespace theater.
- **Not Procore's density.** Information-rich, but not 14-column tables with 8px text. Hierarchy through color, scale, and grouping, not cramming.

## Design Principles

1. **Status at a glance.** Every screen has a single dominant signal: green (go), amber (watch), red (act). No neutral gray ambiguity on critical surfaces.
2. **Decisions, not dashboards.** Surfaces show who owns what and what happens next — not just charts.
3. **One action per view.** When something is red, the next step is one tap away. No hunting through menus.
4. **Proven over novel.** Use patterns field crews and PMs already know (Gantt bars, RFI logs, traffic-light status). Innovation belongs in workflow speed, not in re-inventing basics.
5. **Wear it outside.** Contrast, type size, and touch targets must survive bright sunlight, dust, and work gloves. Elegance is legibility under pressure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA baseline.
- Color-blind safe: status distinctions reinforced by shape, label, or icon — never color alone.
- Reduced motion respected: no animation gating content visibility.
- Touch targets minimum 44×44 dp / px on all interactive elements.
